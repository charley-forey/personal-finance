# Social Security Planning

## Claiming Ages
- **62**: Earliest eligibility, permanently reduced benefit (~30% less than full)
- **67**: Full retirement age for those born 1960+
- **70**: Maximum benefit with delayed credits (~8%/year from FRA to 70)

## Spousal Benefits
A lower-earning spouse may claim up to 50% of the higher earner's benefit at full retirement age.

## Taxation
Up to 85% of benefits may be taxable depending on combined income (AGI + nontaxable interest + half of SS benefits).

## Break-Even Analysis
Delaying from 62 to 70 typically breaks even around age 80–82. Longer life expectancy favors delaying.
