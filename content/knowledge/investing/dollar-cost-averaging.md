# Dollar-Cost Averaging

## What It Is
Investing a fixed amount at regular intervals regardless of market price. Buys more shares when prices are low, fewer when high.

## Benefits
- Reduces timing risk
- Builds investing discipline
- Smooths entry into volatile markets

## Lump Sum vs DCA
Historical data shows lump-sum investing outperforms DCA ~68% of the time over 10-year periods. However, DCA reduces regret and behavioral risk.

## Best For
- New investors entering the market
- Large windfalls (inheritance, bonus) where full immediate investment feels risky
- 401(k) contributions (automatic DCA)

*Past performance does not guarantee future results.*
