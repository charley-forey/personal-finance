# Power of Attorney

## Types
- **Financial POA**: Authorizes someone to manage finances, pay bills, and handle accounts.
- **Healthcare POA / proxy**: Allows medical decisions if you're incapacitated.
- **Durable**: Remains effective if you become incapacitated — essential for estate planning.

## Choosing an Agent
Pick someone trustworthy, financially responsible, and willing to serve. Name alternates in case your first choice cannot act.

## Springing vs Immediate
**Immediate** POA takes effect when signed. **Springing** activates only upon incapacity — harder to use but offers more control while competent.

## Scope
Can be broad or limited to specific tasks. Review with an attorney to match your needs.

## Revocation
You can revoke a POA while competent. Keep copies with financial institutions and update them when agents change.
