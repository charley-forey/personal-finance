# Estate Tax Basics

## Federal Exemption
The federal estate tax applies to estates exceeding the lifetime exemption (indexed annually). Most estates fall below the threshold and owe no federal estate tax.

## What's Included
Gross estate includes real estate, investments, retirement accounts, life insurance proceeds (if you own the policy), and business interests.

## Portability
A surviving spouse may use a deceased spouse's unused exemption with a timely filed estate return — effectively doubling the exemption for married couples.

## State Estate Taxes
Some states impose separate estate or inheritance taxes at lower thresholds. Know your state's rules if you have significant assets.

## Planning Strategies
Gifting, trusts, charitable giving, and life insurance trusts can reduce taxable estates for high-net-worth households.
