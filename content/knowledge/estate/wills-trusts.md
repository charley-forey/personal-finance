# Wills and Trusts

## Will Basics
A will directs asset distribution after death and names guardians for minor children. Without one, state intestacy laws decide who inherits.

## Revocable Living Trust
Holds assets during life and transfers them at death without probate. Useful in states with lengthy probate or for privacy. You remain trustee while alive.

## Probate
Court-supervised process to validate a will and distribute assets. Can be slow and public. Trusts and beneficiary designations often bypass probate.

## When to Update
Review after marriage, divorce, births, deaths, major asset changes, or moving to a new state.

## Professional Help
Complex estates, blended families, and business interests warrant an estate attorney.
