# Life Insurance Basics

## Why It Matters
Life insurance replaces income for dependents if you die prematurely. It covers mortgage payments, childcare, education, and daily expenses your family would otherwise struggle to fund.

## Term vs Permanent
- **Term life**: Coverage for a set period (10–30 years). Lower cost, best for most families with temporary needs.
- **Whole/universal life**: Lifetime coverage with a cash value component. Higher premiums; useful for estate planning in specific cases.

## How Much Coverage
A common rule: 10–12× annual income, adjusted for existing savings, employer coverage, and debts. Subtract assets that would transfer to heirs.

## When to Buy
Major life events: marriage, first child, new mortgage. Rates rise with age and health — lock in coverage while young and healthy.

## Employer vs Individual
Group policies through work are often insufficient and not portable. Supplement with an individual term policy you own.
