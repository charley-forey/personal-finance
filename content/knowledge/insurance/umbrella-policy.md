# Umbrella Liability Insurance

## What It Covers
Umbrella policies provide extra liability coverage above auto and homeowners limits. Protects assets if you're sued for bodily injury or property damage.

## Who Needs It
Consider umbrella coverage if you have significant assets, own rental property, have teenage drivers, or engage in activities with liability exposure (pools, dogs, hosting events).

## Typical Limits
Policies start at $1 million, often available up to $5–10 million. Cost is relatively low compared to coverage amount — often $150–300/year per $1M.

## How It Works
Primary policies (auto/home) pay first up to their limits; umbrella kicks in for amounts above that, up to the umbrella limit.

## Asset Protection
Umbrella insurance complements — not replaces — adequate primary coverage and sound risk management.
