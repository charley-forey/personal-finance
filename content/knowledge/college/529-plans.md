# 529 College Savings Plans

## How They Work
State-sponsored accounts for education expenses. Contributions grow tax-free; withdrawals for qualified education costs are tax-free.

## Qualified Expenses
Tuition, fees, books, room and board (with enrollment requirements), and up to $10,000/year for K–12 tuition in most states.

## Investment Options
Age-based portfolios automatically shift to conservative allocations as the beneficiary approaches college age.

## Tax Benefits
Many states offer income tax deductions or credits for contributions. No federal deduction, but tax-free growth is the main benefit.

## Financial Aid Impact
529 assets owned by parents count at up to 5.64% in Expected Family Contribution — less impact than student-owned assets.
