# Student Budgeting

## Fixed vs Variable Costs
Track tuition, housing, meal plans, and fees separately from entertainment, dining out, and travel.

## Monthly Budget Framework
Income (work-study, part-time job, family support) minus essentials (rent, food, transport, phone) equals discretionary spending.

## Avoiding Debt Traps
Use student discounts, buy used textbooks, limit credit card use, and borrow only what you need for education — not lifestyle.

## Emergency Buffer
Keep $500–$1,000 in a savings account for unexpected expenses like car repairs or medical copays.

## Tools
Use budgeting apps, separate accounts for spending categories, and review transactions weekly to stay on track.
